Six directions for serrevue.

project   serrevue.com scope   primary mark + wordmark palette   sage · forest · cream · honey

Serre + vue.  A greenhouse you can talk to — and that watches over itself. Every direction below pulls on one of those threads: the seeing, the growing, or the conversation between them.

Marks are designed to hold up at favicon size, work in single colour, and feel calm and confident rather than techy. Pick one, mix two, or send notes back — these are sketches, not commitments.

01 VEUE eye + leaf
SERREVUE

An eye that also reads as a leaf.

The almond is a vesica piscis — the simplest shape that is unmistakably both a leaf and an eye. The seed at the centre is the iris, the pupil, the grain of intelligence at the core of the system.
02 BLOOM LENS aperture + flower
SERREVUE

A camera aperture, but it's a flower.

Five petals reading equally as the blades of a lens and the petals of a bloom. The dot at the centre is the focal point — and the seed. Best mark if the brand wants to lean into the AI-vision side without losing warmth.
03 SERRE greenhouse + iris
SERREVUE

The greenhouse is watching.

The classic gable-roof silhouette of a serre, with a single iris inside. The most literal of the six — and the most reassuring. The mark says greenhouse first, technology second.
04 SPROUT letterform S + leaves
SERREVUE

The letter S as a young plant.

Two opposing leaves meet at a stem — together they trace the silhouette of an S. Strongest as a single-letter mark for app icons, t-shirts, and merch. The seed-bud in the middle gives it a clear centre.
05 DIALOGUE leaf + speech bubble
SERREVUE

A leaf with something to say.

Speech-bubble tail tucked into a leaf body, with three veins reading as text lines. The most product-true of the bunch — it points directly at “talk to your greenhouse.” Friendly, warm, slightly editorial.
06 PULSE wordmark + live dot
serrevue
SERREVUE

Wordmark-led, with a living dot.

Mixed typography: serre in Space Grotesk, vue in Fraunces italic — a small typographic nod to the bilingual story. The single pulsing dot is the brand's heartbeat, already echoed throughout the product UI.
Palette
The marks are designed in a single weight of sage. Honey appears only as an accent on the product, never on the logo.
Typography
Wordmark in Space Grotesk SemiBold, slightly tightened tracking. The “vue” half can swap to Fraunces Italic in editorial contexts to lean into the French / artisanal feel.
